Paying for a postsecondary education (college or trade school) is an investment.

It requires planning. It takes money. So talk with your family openly and early. Tuition

What is the federal government’s role in inancial aid?

he federal government is the main provider of inancial aid for college. Every year, students and parents receive more than $150 billion in federal student aid. With all these funds available, applying for aid from the federal government is a good option. Federal student aid from the federal government includes:

  • ｴ grants—free money that doesn’t have to be repaid, except in some cases when you withdraw from school;

  • ｴ work-study—money you earn to pay for your education;

  • ｴ loans—money you borrow for school, which you must repay with interest.
